Driving for Vacants YOU CAN GET PAID TO DRIVE FOR VACANT HOMES! Driving for vacant/abandoned properties is easy, and probably something you are already doing! Read through the information below to find out what exactly to look for. What to look for: When driving around you are looking for obvious signs of vacancy- including single family homes, multifamily (up to 4 units in one building) and commercial property (apartment complexes). Good indicators that a home is vacant: Boarded up or smashed out windows High grass Mailboxes piled up or collection of newspapers in yard No curtains or blinds in windows Condemned, eviction + foreclosure notices Code Violations Obvious empty lots among other homes Clearly empty houses Trees down in yard or driveway House looks to be in need of repair or worse condition than surrounding homes Gutters are falling off SUBMIT A VACANT PROPERTY Tips + Tricks: Talk to neighbors you see outside. Often times they have a lot of information to give (even phone numbers) and are happy to tell you because they do not want an abandoned home next door to them. Politely ask them if they know anything about the house. If you can see the backyard or back of the house, it usually helps show if a property is vacant/abandoned or not. Driving on trash day or during holidays can help determine what properties are vacant. Vacant homes will not have their trash on the curb nor holiday decorations. Check for utility meters. If there is no water or electric meter on the property, more than likely the home is vacant. In general, the best time to drive is from 10:00 a.m. to early afternoon during the weekday. At this time, people are typically at work and it makes it easier to take your time driving through subdivisions. What do you need to get started? Internet Access Camera As you drive the neighborhoods and locate distressed properties, record the address and any additional notes (example: What signs of vacancy does the property show?). Take a picture or two of the property as well. Try and take the “best” picture of the property as possible.
